Year 1 Volume 1


Classroom of the Elite begins at the prestigious Tokyo Metropolitan Advanced Nurturing High School, an institution renowned for producing Japan’s future leaders. The school offers students a seemingly utopian environment with luxurious facilities, a monthly allowance in points that functions as currency, and complete freedom in managing their lives. However, this freedom comes with a catch: everything is determined by performance, and students must compete to earn points, privileges, and status. We meet Kiyotaka Ayanokōji, an enigmatic and introverted student placed in Class D, the lowest-ranked class known for housing "problematic" students. Initially blending into the background, Kiyotaka observes his ambitious yet socially awkward classmate, Suzune Horikita, who is determined to rise to Class A. The cheerful and seemingly perfect Kikyo Kushida befriends both Kiyotaka and Horikita, though her true intentions remain unclear. As the class begins to unravel the school’s merit-based system, Kiyotaka’s sharp intellect and manipulative skills subtly come into play, hinting at a hidden genius who may be far more than he seems. This volume sets the stage for a thrilling series filled with psychological intrigue and high-stakes competition.

Year 1 Volume 2

Class D faces its first major challenge as the summer break looms. The school announces a special exam, testing the students’ ability to work together under pressure. The exam forces the class to collaborate, a task made difficult by the internal divisions and conflicting personalities that plague Class D. Suzune Horikita steps up as the de facto leader, attempting to guide the class, but her aloof nature and lack of social skills create tension among her peers. Meanwhile, Kiyotaka quietly observes the chaos, subtly influencing the class’s decisions to prevent outright failure. His behind-the-scenes maneuvering ensures that Class D can maintain its standing, but it also raises questions about his true abilities and motivations. This volume introduces the school’s harsh testing methods and highlights the growing dynamic between Kiyotaka, Horikita, and their classmates.

Year 1 Volume 3

What should have been a relaxing summer vacation aboard a luxury cruise ship quickly turns into another high-stakes challenge. The school announces a special exam focused on deception and strategy, where students must form alliances and identify the "VIP" among their group. Trust is tested as rivalries between the classes intensify, and the students must navigate a game of manipulation and deduction. Kiyotaka finds himself in a group with students from other classes, observing their dynamics while subtly guiding the outcome. The volume explores the social complexities of the school’s system while deepening the rivalries and friendships among the characters. It’s a thrilling and cerebral entry in the series.

The rules of the test require each class to manage their limited supplies, solve challenges, and identify the leader of opposing classes to earn points. This volume is a showcase of Kiyotaka’s intellect and cunning, as well as a turning point for the series, where the characters begin to realize the harsh realities of their environment.

Year 1 Volume 8

Classroom of the Elite is a popular Japanese light novel series written by Shōgo Kinugasa and illustrated by Shunsaku Tomose. It began serialization in 2015 through Media Factory’s MF Bunko J imprint. The series quickly gained a large following due to its unique exploration of meritocracy and psychological warfare in a fictional high school setting, the Tokyo Metropolitan Advanced Nurturing School, which trains Japan's future elites. Story and Themes The narrative follows Kiyotaka Ayanokōji, a highly intelligent but reserved student in Class-D, a section for students deemed “inferior.” The school operates on a strict meritocratic system where students can earn or lose privileges based on their academic and social performance. This intense, competitive environment brings out manipulative, ambitious, and resilient characteristics in students, creating a suspenseful and psychologically rich storyline. Year 1 Volume 9

The third semester kicks off with rising tensions among the classes, particularly between Class B, led by the charismatic Honami Ichinose, and Class A, under the command of the cunning and calculating Arisu Sakayanagi. Sakayanagi initiates a smear campaign against Ichinose, spreading malicious rumors about her past and eroding her reputation within the school. Class B, usually united under Ichinose's leadership, begins to falter as doubts and insecurities creep in. Meanwhile, Ichinose herself grows increasingly distant, struggling to maintain her composure in the face of relentless attacks. Kiyotaka, observing the situation, decides to intervene—not out of altruism, but because Ichinose's fall could disrupt the balance of power in the school. His subtle yet decisive moves help restore her confidence and counter Sakayanagi's schemes, demonstrating his unparalleled ability to manipulate events from behind the scenes. This volume delves deeply into the psychological toll of the school’s brutal environment, exploring themes of resilience, reputation, and the cost of leadership. It also marks a significant shift in Kiyotaka’s approach as he begins to involve himself more actively in the conflicts shaping the school's hierarchy.

Year 1 Volume 10

As the third semester progresses, the school announces a new special exam, this time focusing on ethics and decision-making under pressure. Each class is given moral dilemmas and forced to choose between sacrificing individuals or risking collective failure. The test not only evaluates intelligence but also exposes the true character of each student. For Class D, the stakes are higher than ever. Suzune Horikita steps up as the leader, determined to prove her worth and solidify her position within the class. Meanwhile, Ryūen Kakeru begins plotting his next move, still reeling from his defeat at Kiyotaka's hands. This volume also introduces new dynamics within the other classes, particularly as Class A and Class C attempt to manipulate the results in their favor. Kiyotaka, as always, remains an enigmatic force in the background, subtly steering his classmates while continuing to maintain his unassuming persona. The dilemmas presented in this exam force the students to confront their values and ambitions, creating a narrative filled with tension, strategy, and moral ambiguity.

Year 1 Volume 11

The first-year students face their most grueling challenge yet: a special exam that not only tests their academic and social skills but also introduces the real threat of expulsion. The school increases the pressure by adding new rules designed to exploit the weaknesses of each class, forcing the students to adapt quickly or risk catastrophic failure. For Class D, the exam becomes a make-or-break moment. Kiyotaka Ayanokōji begins to take a more active role, using his intellect and manipulative skills to ensure the class’s survival. Meanwhile, Horikita faces increasing resistance from within Class D as she struggles to maintain unity under immense pressure. The exam also highlights the growing rivalry between Kiyotaka and Nagumo Miyabi, the ambitious student council president who begins to see Kiyotaka as a serious threat. This volume is a masterful blend of high-stakes drama and psychological battles, showcasing the lengths the students will go to protect their place in the school. Kiyotaka’s calculated moves and the unexpected twists in the exam make this one of the most thrilling and intense entries in the series.
